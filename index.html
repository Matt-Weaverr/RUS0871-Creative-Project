Artist's Statement - Matthew Weaver

The inspiration for this is ideas expressed through the non-objective approach to art taken by Kazimir Malevich and the industrial idealism represented by El Lissitzky. It was intriguing to me how an elementary form such as a square, circle, or wedge could represent an entire country or a philosophical notion. I found myself particularly interested in Lissitzky’s Prouns as my guide, I wanted to see whether the static energy of a propaganda poster could be recreated in a dynamic web page.

This is a digital representation of the Russian Avant-Garde. The colors used are red, black, gold, and beige which are similar to the lithographs of Constructivists. The Red Circle is used as the central part of the artwork which symbolizes the collective soul of the nation. The Black Square symbolizes Malevich’s Zero Point where art starts and ends. The Triangles are used to show the influence of Red Wedge which symbolizes the erratic and sharp energy of the individual. The diagonal planks are the girders of constructivist construction in this artwork. By restraining them to one axis only, I am referring to the rigidness of Russian Machine Age in 1920s. Using all these shapes was my attempt at participating in the tradition of Russian polyphonic abstractionism.

The medium for this is an Interactive Web Page. Instead of presenting works of art via a webpage, the webpage itself is the art. The chosen genre of Neo-Constructivism which relies heavily on interactivity

For the process, I was interested in the weight of objects. I wanted to make sure the motion of the objects wasn’t perfect. I used Interpolation and delay to provide each object with its unique motion style. For example, the red circle was made to have high inertia to illustrate the movement of history which is heavy. The black square was given a pull into the center representing the gravity of tradition. The triangles were forced to flee away from the cursor representing the struggle between desire for freedom and outer powers.
